Welcome to BlackJack’s apartment & Self-introduction.

This is my first blog recording and sharing my work at NSC and ENCCS.

1. HPC AE @ NSC & RSE @ ENCCS

From June 1st, 2023, I mostly left academia and officially joined the National Supercomputer Center (NSC), which is hosted by Linköping University (LiU). As an HPC AE (high performance computing application expert) at NSC, my main work is to provide user support at computational materials science and high performance computing areas including

  • answering questions on specific calculations
  • compiling, installing and benchmarking professioal softwares
  • tuning, optimizing, and parallelizing source code
  • helping with workflow for analysis of simulation output and data management
  • creating tutorials and manuals
  • organizing workshops

In the meantime, I also work at ENCCS as an RSE (research software engineer) to

  • provide high-performance computing training and support for industry, academia and public administration for their access to HPC computational resources in Europe
  • support and further enhance national strengths in high-performance computing, high-performance data analytics, and artificial intelligence (AI)
  • assist key user communities in enabling applications on (pre)exa-scale computational resources
  • advise users to achieve scaling and performance for scientific problems on EuroHPC resources
  • support new AI/data analytics applications
  • provide training and skill development nationally and throughout Europe

EuroCC National Competence Center Sweden (ENCCS) is part of the EuroCC project, which leads the initiative to form and coordinate competence centres in high-performance computing across European countries.

ENCCS is hosted by RISE Research Institutes of Sweden and LiU, and is funded by Swedish Research Council (Vetenskapsrådet), Swedish Innovation Agency (Vinnova), and EuroHPC JU.

EuroHPC JU is a multi-billion Euro joint undertaking which enables participating countries to coordinate their efforts and share resources in order to build a world-class supercomputing infrastructure and a competitive innovation ecosystem in supercomputing technologies, applications, and skills in Europe.
